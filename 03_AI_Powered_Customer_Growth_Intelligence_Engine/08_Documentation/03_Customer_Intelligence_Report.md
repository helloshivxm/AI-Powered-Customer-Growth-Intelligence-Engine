# Customer Segmentation Analysis

## Objective

The objective of customer segmentation was to group customers based on purchasing behavior and business value using RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary Value) analysis.

Customer segmentation helps businesses identify valuable customers, improve retention strategies, and allocate resources more effectively.

---

## RFM Analysis Overview

Three customer behavior dimensions were used:

### Recency

Measures how recently a customer made a purchase.

### Frequency

Measures how often a customer makes purchases.

### Monetary Value

Measures the total revenue generated by a customer.

Together, these indicators provide a comprehensive view of customer engagement and business value.

---

## Customer Segment Distribution

The analysis identified five distinct customer segments.

| Segment | Customers |
|----------|----------:|
| At Risk | 3,254 |
| Lost Customers | 1,449 |
| Others | 978 |
| Loyal Customers | 137 |
| Champions | 60 |

### Key Observation

A significant proportion of customers were classified as At Risk or Lost Customers, indicating substantial opportunities for customer retention and re-engagement initiatives.

---

## Key Findings

### Champions

Champions represent the highest-value customer group.

These customers generate significant revenue and demonstrate strong purchasing activity.

### Loyal Customers

Loyal Customers consistently engage with the business and contribute stable long-term value.

These customers represent strong opportunities for upselling and cross-selling initiatives.

### At Risk Customers

At Risk customers previously demonstrated meaningful engagement but show signs of declining activity.

Retention campaigns should prioritize this group.

### Lost Customers

Lost Customers exhibit low engagement and limited purchasing activity.

The cost of re-acquisition should be evaluated before investing significant marketing resources.

### Others

This segment contains customers with moderate engagement levels who may move into higher-value categories through targeted engagement efforts.

Develop loyalty programs and personalized engagement strategies for Champions and Loyal Customers.

# Customer Lifetime Value Analysis

---

- High Value Customers

# Cohort Analysis

## Objective

The objective of Cohort Analysis was to understand customer retention behavior over time and identify patterns in repeat purchasing activity.

Cohort Analysis helps businesses measure how effectively customers are retained after their initial purchase and provides insight into long-term customer engagement.

---

# Cohort Analysis Overview

Customers were grouped based on the month of their first purchase.

Each cohort was then tracked over time to measure how many customers continued purchasing in subsequent months.

This approach allows businesses to evaluate retention performance across different customer groups and time periods.

---

# Retention Trends

The cohort analysis revealed clear retention patterns across customer groups.

# Initial Retention Decline

Most customer cohorts experienced a significant decline in retention during the first few months following acquisition.

This indicates that the highest risk of customer churn occurs shortly after the initial purchase.

# Retention Stabilization

After the initial decline, customer retention generally stabilized between 15% and 30% for many cohorts.

This suggests that customers who remain active beyond the early stages are more likely to continue engaging with the business.

# Strong Long-Term Cohorts

The December 2009 customer cohort demonstrated stronger long-term retention performance compared to several other cohorts.

This cohort maintained customer engagement over an extended period and exhibited relatively stable retention rates.

---

# Key Findings

Customer Churn Occurs Early

Most customer losses occur within the first few months after acquisition.

This highlights the importance of early customer engagement and onboarding initiatives.

Retained Customers Show Greater Long-Term Value

Customers who remain active after the initial months are more likely to become repeat purchasers and contribute greater long-term business value.

Retention Performance Varies Across Cohorts

Different customer cohorts demonstrated different retention patterns, indicating that acquisition quality and customer experiences may vary over time.

Retention Is a Critical Growth Driver

Improving customer retention has the potential to generate significant business value without increasing customer acquisition costs.

---

# Business Recommendations

Improve Customer Onboarding

Create a stronger onboarding experience during the first customer interactions.

A positive early experience can significantly improve retention rates.

Launch Early Retention Campaigns

Develop targeted retention campaigns within the first 90 days after customer acquisition.

This period represents the highest risk of customer churn.

### Introduce Loyalty Programs

Reward repeat customers through loyalty incentives, exclusive benefits, and personalized offers.

### Monitor Retention Monthly

Track retention performance continuously to identify emerging churn risks and evaluate the effectiveness of retention initiatives.

### Focus on High-Potential Cohorts

Analyze high-performing cohorts to identify best practices that can be applied to future customer acquisition strategies.

---

# Conclusion

Cohort Analysis revealed that customer retention is one of the most important drivers of long-term business growth.

The findings demonstrate that the majority of customer churn occurs shortly after acquisition, while customers who remain active beyond the initial months contribute significantly greater long-term value.

These insights provide a strong foundation for customer retention strategies, loyalty initiatives, and sustainable revenue growth planning.
